# ENVIRA: When Earth Gained a Nervous System

*How Technology Became Nature's Greatest Ally in the Race Against Time*

## About This Book

ENVIRA is a non-fiction book exploring the convergence of environmentalism and technology at a pivotal moment in human history. Through real narratives, verified data, and profiles of actual people and organizations, it maps how sensors, AI, and digital infrastructure are creating an unprecedented planetary monitoring system — and how that system is powering the fight against climate change, biodiversity loss, and ecological collapse.

This is not speculative fiction. Every claim is grounded in peer-reviewed research, institutional data, or documented real-world events.

## Structure

The manuscript is organized into six parts across 18 chapters:

- **Part I: The Crisis** — Where we stand: the environmental data, the economics, the tipping points
- **Part II: The Nervous System** — How Earth learned to see itself: satellites, sensors, AI, digital twins
- **Part III: The Arsenal** — Technologies fighting back: carbon capture, renewables, ecosystem restoration, sustainable agriculture
- **Part IV: The Human Equation** — Jobs, justice, behavior, and the cultural transformation
- **Part V: The Obstacles** — Corporate resistance, policy gaps, and the investment challenge
- **Part VI: The Path Forward** — The critical decade and what each of us can do

## Writing Standards

- All claims cite verifiable sources (IPCC, IEA, IRENA, peer-reviewed journals, etc.)
- No fictional characters or composite narratives
- Real people quoted with attribution
- Speculative statements clearly labeled as scenarios or projections
- Opening scenes based on documented real events
- See `manuscript/style-guide.md` for full conventions
