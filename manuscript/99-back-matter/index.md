# Index

**Format:** Comprehensive subject index to be compiled from the final manuscript. This file documents the indexing plan and key entry categories.

---

## Indexing Plan

**Method:** Professional indexer (recommended for a work of this length and reference density). Budget 4–6 weeks after the final manuscript is locked.

**Alternative:** Author-generated index using indexing software (SKY Index, IndexManager, or embedded index markers in Word/InDesign). This approach is significantly more time-intensive but gives the author full control over emphasis and cross-referencing.

**Estimated scope:** ~5,000 index entries for a 148,500-word book with high technical and reference density.

---

## Key Entry Categories

The index should comprehensively cover the following categories:

### Technologies and Technical Concepts
- All 100 technologies from Appendix A, with references to both the appendix entry and any chapter discussion
- Scientific concepts (carbon budget, tipping points, planetary boundaries, albedo, AMOC, etc.)
- Technical methods (direct air capture, enhanced weathering, SAR imaging, eDNA sampling, etc.)

### Organizations and Institutions
- Companies profiled or discussed (Climeworks, Planet Labs, Climate TRACE, etc.)
- International bodies (IPCC, IEA, IRENA, UNEP, UNFCCC)
- NGOs and advocacy organizations
- Research institutions and universities

### People
- Scientists, founders, policymakers, and activists mentioned by name
- Authors of key referenced studies

### Policy and Governance
- Legislation (Inflation Reduction Act, EU Green Deal, Paris Agreement, etc.)
- Policy instruments (carbon pricing, cap-and-trade, renewable portfolio standards, etc.)
- Regulatory bodies and frameworks (SEC climate disclosure, TCFD, ISSB, etc.)

### Geographic Locations
- Countries and regions discussed in case studies
- Specific sites (Climeworks Orca facility in Iceland, Kalundborg industrial symbiosis, etc.)

### Thematic Entries
- Environmental justice
- Just transition
- Greenwashing
- Stranded assets
- Tipping points (cross-referenced with specific systems: AMOC, Amazon, permafrost, ice sheets)
- Nervous system metaphor (cross-referenced across all parts)

---

## Cross-Reference Strategy

The index should use extensive "see also" cross-references to connect related entries across the book's six parts. Examples:

- *Carbon removal* — see also *direct air capture; enhanced weathering; biochar; BECCS; ocean alkalinity enhancement*
- *Monitoring* — see also *satellites; sensors; AI; Climate TRACE; nervous system metaphor*
- *Energy transition* — see also *solar; wind; batteries; grid modernization; heat pumps; green hydrogen*
- *Environmental justice* — see also *just transition; energy democracy; pollution burden; Global South*

---

## Production Notes

- Index cannot begin until the manuscript is final and paginated
- Allow 4–6 weeks for professional indexing
- Review pass by author after initial index draft (1–2 weeks)
- Final index typically adds 15–25 pages to the printed book
- Ensure publishing contract includes index in production timeline and budget
