# Appendix D: Global Directory

**Target word count:** ~2,000 words
**Format:** Organizations, hubs, and frameworks organized by type. Each entry includes: name, description, focus area, website, and notes on relevance to readers.
**Note:** This is a reference directory, not a comprehensive list. Organizations were selected for their relevance to the book's themes and their accessibility to readers seeking further engagement.

---

## 1. International Bodies

Organizations operating at the global level to coordinate climate and environmental action.

| # | Name | Description | Focus | Website |
|---|------|-------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | **UNFCCC** (United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change) | The international treaty and its secretariat that provides the framework for global climate negotiations, including the annual COP conferences | International climate policy, Paris Agreement implementation, National Determined Contributions (NDCs) | unfccc.int |
| 2 | **IPCC** (Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change) | UN body that assesses the science related to climate change through comprehensive reports produced by thousands of scientists | Climate science assessment, impacts, adaptation, and mitigation pathways | ipcc.ch |
| 3 | **IRENA** (International Renewable Energy Agency) | Intergovernmental organization supporting countries in their transition to sustainable energy | Renewable energy policy, technology, finance, and deployment data | irena.org |
| 4 | **IEA** (International Energy Agency) | Autonomous intergovernmental organization providing data, analysis, and policy recommendations on the global energy sector | Energy data and statistics, technology analysis, policy recommendations, Net Zero Roadmap | iea.org |
| 5 | **UNEP** (United Nations Environment Programme) | The leading global environmental authority that sets the global environmental agenda | Environmental assessment, policy guidance, Emissions Gap Report, adaptation gap analysis | unep.org |
| 6 | **WMO** (World Meteorological Organization) | UN specialized agency for meteorology, climate, hydrology, and related geophysical sciences | Weather and climate monitoring, State of the Global Climate report | wmo.int |
| 7 | **GEF** (Global Environment Facility) | Financial mechanism serving multiple environmental conventions | Biodiversity, climate change, land degradation, chemicals/waste funding in developing countries | thegef.org |
| 8 | **CBD** (Convention on Biological Diversity) | International treaty for conservation and sustainable use of biological diversity | Biodiversity conservation, Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework | cbd.int |

## Appendix Notes

**Selection criteria:** Organizations were selected based on: (1) prominence and credibility in their domain, (2) relevance to topics covered in this book, (3) accessibility to readers seeking further engagement or information. This is not an exhaustive list.

**Updates:** The organizational landscape evolves continuously. Readers are encouraged to use the listed websites as starting points and to follow cross-references between organizations for the most current information.

**Regional resources:** This directory emphasizes global and English-language organizations. Readers are encouraged to seek out regional and local equivalents relevant to their geography and context.
