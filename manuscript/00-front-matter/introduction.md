## What "Earth Gaining a Nervous System" Means

The central metaphor of the book, grounded in specifics. For the first time in history, humanity has the technological capacity to monitor the planet's vital signs in real time — CO2 concentrations, ocean temperatures, forest cover, ice sheet mass, biodiversity indicators — and process that data with AI systems that can detect patterns and predict outcomes.

### Key Points to Cover
- The scale of Earth observation today: number of active environmental satellites, ground-based sensor networks, ocean monitoring buoys
- Historical comparison: what Rachel Carson had to work with vs. what scientists have today
- The transition from blind management to informed management of planetary systems
- This is not metaphor — it's infrastructure

### Key Data Points
- ESA Copernicus program: 6 Sentinel satellite families providing continuous Earth observation
- NASA Earth Observing System: 20+ active missions
- Argo float network: 4,000+ autonomous ocean profilers worldwide
- Ground-based sensor networks: millions of IoT environmental sensors deployed globally

## How to Read This Book

The book works in three registers simultaneously:
1. **Narrative**: Real stories of real people and places
2. **Data**: Verified numbers and trends from authoritative sources
3. **Action**: What can be done, by whom, and how

Readers can move linearly or jump to sections most relevant to their interests. Each chapter stands alone while contributing to the whole.
