## CRITICAL ERRORS (Must Fix)

### 1. Canadian wildfire CO2 emissions — Chapter 1
- **Manuscript says:** "6.4 billion tonnes of CO2 equivalent released"
- **Actual:** ~2.4–3.3 billion tonnes CO2 (Nature 2024 study: ~647 TgC ≈ 2.4 Gt CO2; WRI estimate: ~3 Gt CO2)
- **The 6.4 Gt figure is roughly 2x the actual emissions and has no credible source**
- **Fix:** Replace with "approximately 2.4–3 billion tonnes of CO2" and cite the specific source

### 2. Swiss Re GDP projection — Chapter 2
- **Manuscript says:** "up to 23% GDP reduction under ~3.2°C warming"
- **Actual:** The global figure is **up to 18%**. The ~23% applies specifically to **China** only
- **Fix:** Either say "up to 18% global GDP reduction" or specify "up to 23% for China"

### 3. Pakistan 2022 floods GDP impact — Chapter 2
- **Manuscript says:** "~9% of GDP"
- **Actual:** Direct GDP impact was ~2.2% of FY22 GDP. The $30B figure is total damages+losses, which is ~8-9% of GDP arithmetically but conflates different economic concepts
- **Fix:** Clarify: "$30 billion in total damages and economic losses" without the 9% GDP claim, or explain the methodology

### 4. AMOC weakening citation — Chapter 3
- **Manuscript says:** "Caesar et al., Nature Geoscience, 2021"
- **Actual:** The ~15% weakening figure comes from **Caesar et al., 2018, Nature** (not 2021)
- **Fix:** Correct citation to Caesar et al., 2018, Nature

### 5. Greenland ice loss — Chapter 3
- **Manuscript says:** "~4,700 Gt of ice (1992-2020)" attributed to "IMBIE team, Nature, 2023"
- **Actual:** Figure should be **~4,890 Gt**; published in **Earth System Science Data** (not Nature)
- **Fix:** Correct both the number and the journal

### 6. Solar LCOE figures — Chapter 8
- **Manuscript says:** "$0.381/kWh (2010) to $0.049/kWh (2023), 87% decline"
- **Actual (IRENA):** **$0.460/kWh (2010) to $0.044/kWh (2023), 90% decline**
- **All three numbers are wrong.** The actual story is even better than stated
- **Fix:** Use IRENA's published figures: $0.460 to $0.044, 90% decline

### 7. Amazon deforestation reduction — Chapter 4
- **Manuscript says:** "68% reduction in Amazon deforestation between 2004 and 2012"
- **Actual:** The reduction was approximately **80%** (27,700 km² to ~4,500 km²)
- **Fix:** Replace 68% with ~80%

### 8. Global tree cover loss — Chapter 4
- **Manuscript says:** "approximately 437 million hectares" (2001-2023)
- **Actual (Global Forest Watch):** **488 million hectares**
- **Fix:** Update to 488 million hectares

### 9. Climate TRACE source count — Chapter 5
- **Manuscript says:** "80,000+ individual emissions sources"
- **Actual:** Launch coverage was ~70,000 sources; current coverage is **352+ million assets**
- **Fix:** Correct to 70,000 at launch, or note the dramatic expansion

### 10. Wetland loss figure — Chapter 9
- **Manuscript says:** "85% of global wetland area lost since 1700"
- **Actual:** A 2023 Nature study (Fluet-Chouinard et al.) found only **~21% loss**. The 85% figure came from older, less rigorous estimates
- **Fix:** Either cite the newer ~21% figure or present the range with both estimates

### 11. MPA fish biomass — Chapter 9
- **Manuscript says:** "670% more fish biomass inside fully protected MPAs (Sala & Giakoumi, 2018)"
- **Actual:** The paper published a **correction**; the correct figure is **570%**
- **Fix:** Use the corrected figure of 570%

### 12. ILO green jobs scenario — Chapter 11
- **Manuscript says:** "Net gain of 24-25 million jobs by 2030 under 1.5C scenario"
- **Actual:** The ILO 2018 report projected 24 million jobs under a **2°C scenario** (not 1.5C), with net gain of 18 million after accounting for 6 million losses
- **Fix:** Correct to 2°C scenario, 24 million new jobs (net 18 million)

### 13. Inside Climate News Pulitzer — Chapter 14
- **Manuscript says:** "ICN won Pulitzer Prize for Public Service (as a finalist, with prize going to AP; ICN won Pulitzer in 2024)"
- **Actual:** ICN won the 2013 Pulitzer for **National Reporting** (not 2024). They were a finalist for 2016 Public Service (AP won). No evidence of a 2024 Pulitzer win
- **Fix:** Correct to: "ICN won the 2013 Pulitzer Prize for National Reporting and was a 2016 finalist for Public Service"

### 14. Climate tech VC peak — Chapter 16
- **Manuscript says:** "peaked at approximately $40 billion in 2022"
- **Actual:** Major trackers report **$50-70 billion** for 2022 (PwC: $50B+; HolonIQ: $70B; BNEF: $59B)
- **Fix:** Update to reflect actual data range (~$50-70 billion depending on methodology)

## STRUCTURAL OBSERVATIONS

### What the Book Does Well
1. **Extensive source lists per chapter** — every chapter has a dedicated Sources section with specific citations
2. **Institutional sources prioritized** — IPCC, IEA, IRENA, NASA, NOAA, FAO, WHO are the backbone
3. **Self-aware about uncertainties** — the manuscript flags controversial studies (Bastin 2019, Ditlevsen 2023) and notes caveats
4. **Style guide enforces rigor** — no fictional characters, speculative language labeled, data standards defined
5. **Company claims distinguished from peer-reviewed data** — the manuscript generally flags when data comes from corporate sources

### What Needs Improvement
1. **Several company-sourced claims lack independent verification** — Plenty's 350x yield, Dendra's 100,000+ seeds/day, various corporate employment and investment figures
2. **The NO2 disparity stat in Chapter 12** has a citation uncertainty noted in the text ("verify exact citation") — this needs resolution. The 56% figure likely comes from Tessum et al. (2019, PNAS), not Colmer et al.
3. **Some rapidly-changing data points** (Climate TRACE sources, GFW vessels, battery costs) will be significantly outdated by publication. Consider using a "as of [date]" convention
4. **The bibliography (back matter)** needs to be compiled from the per-chapter source lists — it's currently described as a placeholder

### Recommendations for Publication Readiness
1. Fix all 14 critical errors before publication
2. Update the 13 outdated figures to the most recent data available at time of final editing
3. Resolve the 4 unverifiable claims — either find primary sources or qualify with "according to [company]"
4. Add "as of [date]" qualifiers to rapidly-changing metrics
5. Complete the bibliography from chapter source lists
6. Verify all "interviews needed" have been conducted or remove references to unnamed individuals
7. Consider a professional fact-checker for final pass, particularly on Chapter 14 (corporate resistance) where legal precision matters
